wardrop-repro · a verification layer for the multi-population Wardrop program

A convergent flow finds the equilibrium. A certificate proves it’s there.

The paper — Bakaryan, Aoun, de Lima Ribeiro, Hovakimyan & Gomes, Distributed Hessian–Riemannian flow for multi-population Wardrop equilibrium, AIMS Mathematics 11(5): 15143–15162 (2026) — computes each equilibrium with a globally convergent flow and validates it against a benchmark: existence-by-algorithm plus a numerical match. This page reproduces those results independently and adds the complementary thing a stopping rule cannot give: a proof. Where the costs are affine (S2) it returns the exact rational equilibrium; where they are nonlinear (S3), a Krawczyk enclosure — an explicit box holding exactly one solution, locally unique; where the instance is degenerate (S1), an honest refusal that displays the non-uniqueness S1 admits — its cost is monotone but not strictly, so the paper’s Theorem 4 (uniqueness under strict monotonicity) does not apply and only the totals are pinned. A stopping rule finds where the equilibrium is; the certificate proves it is there, alone, and positive. S1 and S2 reproduce exactly — the interesting story is S3.

⚠ Where this stands. All three scenarios reproduce: S1 to the digit (Table 1’s (4,7) settles to 52, certified four ways — docs/ERRATA.md), S2 exactly (Figure 2, car and truck), and S3 — the emissions case — to integer rounding against Figure 4, now with a certified Krawczyk enclosure. The instructive part is how S3 got there: matching the figure forced this reproduction to find a bug in its own cost code — a dropped 10⁻³ emission factor (and a congestion-flow slip) — which is exactly what a certificate is for: it catches the implementer’s mistakes, not only the author’s. Under the corrected cost the equilibrium is the same for any edge lengths, so nothing here hinges on reading a figure. The certified equilibria are live below.

S3 is where the certificate caught something — in this reproduction's own code. Matching Figure 4 forced a confrontation with the fact that the reproduction's cost did not agree with the paper's: it had dropped the 10⁻³ emission factor (making the emission term 1000× too large) and used a truck-weighted congestion flow where §6.3 specifies the plain total j¹+j². With the paper-faithful cost, the standard solver converges cleanly and S3 reproduces Figure 4a to integer rounding — and because the emission term is then a small correction, it does so for any edge lengths, so the exact sₖ are immaterial and no figure needs reading. Every earlier symptom (“the lengths don't match”, “the solver stalls”) was this one bug. A certificate that makes the implementer confront a 1000× discrepancy in their own cost function is doing exactly its job.

Where this stands, and what remains

All three scenarios reproduce; S3 is length-independent (nothing here depends on reading a figure) and now carries a certified Krawczyk enclosure — the location-and-local-uniqueness ground truth Table 5 lacks, since there HRF is compared only to itself. Two threads stay open, both recorded honestly. The same 10⁻³ cost bug lives in the public mfg-lab S3 module and its O7 Krawczyk certificate — a genuine fix, held under that track's change-freeze. And a certified statement of the PG–GS–HRF accuracy gap in the emissions case is the natural “bigger scale” extension.

The frontier — scaling the certificate to bigger scenarios prospective · the program

The three scenarios above are a complete certified reproduction of the published instance. The standard they establish — exact where the costs are affine, a Krawczyk enclosure where they are nonlinear, an honest refusal where the instance is degenerate — certifies equilibria, not a particular network size or population count, so it carries to larger scenarios unchanged. What such an expansion needs, stated honestly and badged as not-yet-done — a program, not a claim:

The honest content of this section is that the standard is well-defined and portable, not that these results exist — every row is prospective or open, and none is drawn or tabulated above. A bigger-scenario program scales the certificate; it does not weaken it. Whatever the size, an equilibrium ships exact, enclosed, or honestly refused — never merely “within tolerance.”

What this reproduction will not claim

Never “their table is wrong.” The one genuine paper slip — Table 1's (4,7) row — is a typesetting artifact, stated generously; the reproduction's own claim is only that its totals carry a machine-zero certificate. The one flag raised against a figure (the Fig 4b truck exits) turned out to be a reproducer misread, and was withdrawn.

The certificate certifies the equilibrium, not the optimum. The paper studies Wardrop I only; the VI is not a potential minimizer except under structure it does not impose. Nothing here describes HRF as “minimizing” in S2/S3.

The method is standard; the reproduction and its self-correction are the substance. Krawczyk, verified complementarity and exact rational solves are off the shelf. What is real here: an independent, proof-carrying reproduction of this non-variational multi-population system — one honest enough to find, and fix, a 1000× error in its own cost code.

Nothing grades the HRF solver. The timings and step counts (Tables 2/3/5) are reproduced for the record only — not re-run, not ranked. HRF finds the equilibrium; the certificate proves it. Two layers, not two competitors.

No external referee yet. Every claim here has been through independent internal adversarial review — multiple passes that re-derive each result from scratch and try to break it — but all of it is one studio's own scrutiny, not the external referee it has not yet had.

Every number is regenerated from a headless battery that extracts the solver kernel and prints the sha256 it validated; every check is mutation-tested; the whole page is one file — no libraries, no build step, no network.

Enclosure builds computer-assisted proofs for mean-field games and network equilibria. Where a paper reports a converged iterate, Enclosure returns a certificate — an exact-rational or interval enclosure of the equilibrium, with its location and local uniqueness proved, or an explicit refusal where the instance is degenerate. Certificates over tolerances; a falsifier behind every claim; a green run is evidence precisely because a broken one is built to go red.

For a research group this is a way to validate and stress-test a published result — independently, and in good faith. The same machinery certifies numerical and optimization claims — the kind a frontier system must be able to depend on rather than merely hope for.
